Industry solutions

Start with the process, then match the material

Industrial buyers rarely need a product name alone. They need a material whose chemistry, particle size, physical form, packing and inspection scope fit the operating process.

Application areas
Application note

Application information provides a technical discussion framework. It does not claim existing customer supply, process approval or guaranteed performance. Final suitability must be confirmed against the buyer’s furnace, recipe, standard and trial requirements.

Application areas

Where our four material families may fit

Each application starts with the operating objective and the indicators a procurement or technical team should confirm before quotation.

01

Ferroalloy and silicon smelting

Semi coke is used as a carbon reductant in submerged-arc-furnace routes including ferrosilicon production. Carbon chemistry, electrical behavior, reactivity and burden size all influence furnace suitability.

Confirm before RFQ
  • Target alloy and furnace route
  • Fixed carbon, ash, sulfur and phosphorus limits
  • Particle size and fines tolerance
  • Electrical resistivity or reactivity requirements
Semi Coke / Lan Tan→
02

Calcium carbide production

Semi coke can serve as the carbon feedstock in calcium carbide production. Buyers normally align fixed carbon and impurity limits with furnace burden preparation and product-quality targets.

Confirm before RFQ
  • Furnace and burden preparation
  • Fixed carbon and ash
  • Sulfur and phosphorus limits
  • Size distribution, moisture and fines
Semi Coke / Lan Tan→
03

Iron foundry and steel melting

Carbon powder used as a carburizing material can be reviewed for carbon adjustment in gray iron, ductile iron and cast-steel melting. Product matching depends on the melt route, addition stage, recovery target and impurity limits.

Confirm before RFQ
  • Melt type and addition stage
  • Carbon recovery target
  • Sulfur, ash and volatile-matter limits
  • Particle size, dissolution and trial method
Carbon Powder→
04

Aluminum alloying and magnesium die casting

Primary magnesium is used in aluminum-base alloys and magnesium castings. Grade selection should be based on the buyer’s alloy recipe, impurity limits, melt practice and handling controls.

Confirm before RFQ
  • Alloy designation or recipe
  • Mg grade and impurity limits
  • Ingot dimensions and bundle requirements
  • Melt handling, safety and inspection scope
Magnesium Ingots→
05

Steel desulfurization and metal reduction

Magnesium metal is also used in iron and steel desulfurization and as a reducing agent in metals production, including the Kroll route for titanium. These are process-specific applications requiring technical qualification.

Confirm before RFQ
  • Process route and addition system
  • Purity and impurity limits
  • Form, dimensions and safe handling
  • Sampling, analysis and transaction documents
Magnesium Ingots→
06

Film, molding, pipe and extrusion

PE and PP grade selection begins with the resin family and processing route. Film, injection molding, blow molding, pipe, sheet, fiber and extrusion applications require different flow, density, impact, stiffness, optical and compliance profiles.

Confirm before RFQ
  • Resin family and grade code
  • Processing method and end use
  • MFR or MFI and test condition
  • Additives, color and compliance documents
